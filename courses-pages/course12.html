Wi-Fi Safe Usage Course


Despite Wi-Fi being something accessible and common in people's daily lives, there are many risks that can go unnoticed. Using Wi-Fi offers many conveniences, but it also poses security risks that can compromise your personal data and privacy. Therefore, it is important to pay attention to some points when using Wi-Fi in order to avoid security issues.




Data Theft:


This is one of the biggest risks when using unsecured Wi-Fi. When you connect to an unprotected Wi-Fi network, your data can be intercepted by hackers who are on the same network. This includes confidential information such as passwords, credit card details, browsing history, and personal messages.

Example: When connecting to a public Wi-Fi network in a café, the user decides to access their bank account. A hacker who is also on the same network intercepts the data sent by the user, including their username and password. With this information, the hacker can access the user's bank account and carry out unauthorized transactions.

To protect yourself against data theft, it is essential to use Wi-Fi networks protected by passwords whenever possible. Networks protected by security protocols like WPA2 or WPA3 are harder to breach. Additionally, avoid entering confidential information on unsecured websites and keep your devices and applications updated to fix any known vulnerabilities.


Identity Theft:


Identity theft occurs when hackers gain access to enough personal information to impersonate you. This may include data such as your full name, date of birth, social security number, address, and other personal information.

Example: The user is at the airport and connects to the public Wi-Fi network offered in the terminal. A hacker who is on the same network manages to capture personal information from the user, such as their full name, email address, and phone number, while they browse the internet. With this information, the hacker can easily steal the user's identity and commit fraud.

To protect yourself against identity theft when using Wi-Fi, avoid providing confidential personal information on public networks. Additionally, consider using a VPN to encrypt your connection and protect your data from interception by third parties.


Malware and Phishing Attacks:


Unsecured Wi-Fi networks are often targeted by malware and phishing attacks. Hackers can use these networks to distribute malware to connected devices or send phishing emails designed to deceive users and steal their information.

Example: The user receives an email while connected to the Wi-Fi network in their hotel, informing them that their bank account has been compromised and requesting them to click on a link to "fix" the issue. The link actually leads to a fake website designed to steal the user's banking credentials or install malware on their device.

To protect yourself against malware and phishing, ensure that all devices you use to connect to the internet have up-to-date antivirus and antimalware software. Additionally, be cautious when clicking on links in emails or text messages, especially if they are from unknown senders. Espionage and Monitoring:


In unsecured Wi-Fi networks, hackers can monitor your online activity, including websites you visit, messages you send and receive, and any other information transmitted over the network.

Example: The user is at an airport using the public Wi-Fi network to send confidential business emails. A hacker who is on the same network can use monitoring tools to intercept their emails and obtain confidential information about their business.

To protect yourself against espionage and monitoring, avoid conducting sensitive activities such as financial transactions or accessing online accounts on unsecured public Wi-Fi networks. Additionally, consider using a VPN to encrypt your internet traffic and protect your privacy.


Spoofing Attacks:


Spoofing occurs when hackers create fake Wi-Fi networks with names similar to legitimate ones to deceive users and steal their data.

Example: The user is at an airport and sees two available Wi-Fi networks: "Airport Wi-Fi" and "Free Airport Wi-Fi." They connect to the second option, thinking it is the free network offered by the airport, but it is actually a fake network created by a hacker to capture their data.

To protect yourself against spoofing attacks, always carefully check the name and security of the Wi-Fi network before connecting. Avoid connecting to networks that appear suspicious or do not require a password for access. If in doubt, ask the personnel responsible for the network, such as café or hotel staff, about the correct Wi-Fi network.


Valuable tips for using Wi-Fi securely:

  • Do not join unfamiliar networks.
  • Disable the option to automatically connect to networks to prevent unintentional connection to public, password-less networks.
  • Use a VPN, which encrypts data and hides your real IP address, enhancing your connection security.
  • Avoid logging into any network or service or conducting banking transactions when connected to an unknown Wi-Fi
  • Always log out after using an unfamiliar network.
